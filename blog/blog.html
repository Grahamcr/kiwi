vMOX and Advantage backend redesign - Meg

Thanks again to everyone who participated in the card sort activity! Craig presented the vMOX findings this week and it helped to drive important decisions about the navigation scheme. It took longer to get data on the Advantage side so I just started to analyze the results but so far they are consistent with vMOX. Here again are the vMOX results: vMOX Card Sort Study Results. Craig and I met with the Amigos to discuss the results and several decisions we made. Based the results it was concluded that goal of our redesign is to eventually break out the navigation structure into different microsites that all wrap up to a parent navigation bar. In other words, if you work for sales you would log in and find that you are redirected to vmox-portal.com/sales and are presented with a navigation structure which only includes links relevant to your job responsibilities. If you, as a sales person, need to see something from operations you would go up one level to the parent and then navigate back down to the Procurement microsite.

New/Interesting UX Fact (Meg)

Since I have color on the brain this week, here are interesting UX facts about color. Did you know that Google once tested 41 shades of blue for their links to find out which color people were most likely to click on? You can read more about how to use color when designing here: Intro to Color Theory and here: Tips for choosing colors.

Also, did you know that Mark Zuckerberg is red-green colorblind? He chose blue for Facebook's primary color because it is the color that he can see best. 8% of men are red-green color blind and it is an important factor to consider when designing. You can simulate different kinds of color blindness using ColorOracle; color contrast checkers can also be very helpful.
