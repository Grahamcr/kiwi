vMOX Colors

Created May 22, 2018

Composed by Craig Graham and Megan Graham

Key Points - vMOX Colors:
  • vMOX black and yellow are the primary colors for any vMOX application
  • There are 14 secondary colors used to complement vMOX black and yellow
  • Dark gray is used instead of black for a softer look
  • Accessibility and readability should always be considered when choosing colors

Effective use of color is extremely important for successful web design. Colors are used to convey meaning to users. Status colors take advantage of users' previous associations, for example yellow means caution or slow down. Strategic use of color can draw attention to different parts of a page as well as convey hierarchy and importance. When choosing or combining colors it is extremely important that readibility be preserved. Per accessiblity rules color should not be the only way that information is conveyed; use an icon to draw attention to an error, not just red text.

Primary Colors

vMOX primary colors are used extensively to build and differentiate the vMOX brand. They can be used for large blocks of color, as accent colors and for headings. They should not be used for statistics and status.
